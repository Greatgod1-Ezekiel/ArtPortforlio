THE ARTISTIC PIPELINE

A Transparent Path from
Sketch to Masterpiece.

01. Creative Consultation

We begin by discussing your vision. You provide the reference photos, and we talk about the size, medium (pencil or digital), and any specific emotional "vibe" you want the piece to convey.

02. Technical Planning

I analyze the composition. For pencil works, this involves setting up precise grids. For digital works, I map out the color palette and lighting to ensure the final result is balanced and professional.

03. Meticulous Execution

The actual creation phase. I build layers of graphite or digital paint, focusing first on form and then on the hyper-fine details that make the artwork pop. I prioritize quality over speed during this stage.

04. Review & Final Delivery

Once finished, I present the work for a final review. After any minor refinements, the artwork is professionally packaged or exported in high-definition formats and delivered to you.

FAQS

Everything You Need to Know

What is the difference between your pencil and digital services?

Pencil portraits are physical artworks hand-drawn on premium paper—they offer a classic, textured feel. Digital art is created on a tablet, resulting in a versatile digital file that can be used online or printed on various materials like canvas, wood, or metal multiple times.

Can you work with old or blurry photographs?

Yes, I frequently work on "restoration" style portraits. While a clear photo is always better, I can use my artistic knowledge of anatomy and lighting to reconstruct details that might be missing from an older or lower-quality reference image.

How long does a typical commission take to complete?

Quality takes time. A standard portrait usually takes 7 to 14 days depending on the complexity and my current schedule. If you have a specific deadline (like a birthday), please let me know during the consultation so I can prioritize your project.

INVESTMENT

Pricing & Commission Value

Commissioning a custom artwork is an investment in a legacy that transcends time. My pricing structure is not merely a reflection of materials used, but a valuation of the dozens of hours of meticulous labor, technical expertise, and the emotional depth infused into every stroke. Whether it is a hand-drawn charcoal portrait or a high-end digital painting, each piece is treated as a museum-grade project.

I believe in transparency and quality. Because every face tells a different story and every request carries unique complexities ranging from the number of subjects to the intricacy of the background prices are tailored to the specific needs of your project. Below is a breakdown of how I value my craftsmanship.

Pencil & Charcoal Works

Physical commissions are priced based on size (A4, A3, A2, or custom mural sizes) and the level of hyper-realism required. A single-subject A3 portrait, for instance, requires intensive layering of graphite and carbon to achieve that signature "3D pop."

Factors that influence the final quote include the complexity of clothing (like lace or patterned traditional attire), jewelry details, and whether the background is simple or fully rendered.

Digital Masterpieces

Digital art offers incredible flexibility. Pricing for these sessions covers the creation of high-resolution source files (300 DPI) and the commercial licensing rights often required by influencers and brands.

Since digital works can be reproduced on various surfaces, the cost covers the artistic time spent on color grading, digital "brushwork," and ensuring the file is optimized for large-format canvas printing without loss of quality.

Note on Custom Projects: For memorial restorations, multi-subject family trees, or conceptual brand illustrations, I provide a bespoke quote after our initial consultation. A commitment deposit is required to secure your slot in my artistic pipeline, ensuring dedicated time is reserved exclusively for your masterpiece.

FEATURED MASTERPIECE

“Example Of Leadership”

This portrait was created as a tribute to the Executive Governor of Abia State, Dr. Alex Otti, whose leadership and developmental vision have continued to inspire many across the state and beyond. Through this artwork, I wanted to capture not only his physical appearance, but also the sense of determination, calm strength, and progressive energy that has defined his administration.

Over the past few years, Abia State has witnessed visible transformation in infrastructure, road development, environmental sanitation, governance structure, and public confidence. This piece reflects the spirit of renewal and leadership that many people associate with his administration. Every detail in the portrait was intentionally crafted to communicate authority, vision, and resilience.

Hours Invested

This artwork required over 10 hours of detailed pencil rendering, layering, shading refinement, and structural corrections to achieve a hyper-realistic finish worthy of a leadership tribute piece.

Materials Used

The portrait was created using professional graphite pencils, charcoal pencils, blending tools, precision erasers, and premium textured paper designed for archival-quality realism and long-term preservation.

Meaning Behind The Artwork

Beyond realism, this piece symbolizes progress, discipline, and transformation. The intention was to create a portrait that represents leadership with depth and emotional presence rather than simply recreating a photograph.

Creative Challenges

One of the greatest challenges was balancing hyper-realistic detailing while preserving natural expression and emotional authenticity. Achieving proper skin texture, fabric depth, and realistic lighting required multiple refinement stages.
